Herman Hesse

- Born in Germany, spent youth in india w/ chrisitan missionary
- Inner conflict btw christianity and eastern religions
- Went to seminary school
- Left after attemting suicide
- First published in 1896 (poetry)
- WWI sees his wife's mental health deteriorate
- In 1916 she has mental breakdown, his father dies, and his son gets sick
- Sought help outside of christianity, in spirituality and eastern religion
- Becomes patient of student of Carl Jung
- Divorce & remarries (x2)

- Example of a seeker; always looking for meaning
- Demian, published in 1919, was popular among youth
- Goes against societal norms
